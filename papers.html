  • Quantum many-body physics: Classical and Quantum-Circuit Benchmarks for Dynamical Correlations in a 2D Heisenberg Magnet
    Quantum processors are increasingly being explored as tools for simulating many-body dynamics, but physics-facing benchmarks must go beyond circuit fidelity and target experimentally meaningful observables. We propose an observable-level benchmark programme for dynamical spin correlations in small two-dimensional quantum magnets. The benchmark compares exact diagonalization and Krylov time evolution, MPS/TDVP under two-dimensional snake mappings, and Trotterized quantum-circuit simulation on common observables such as low-energy spectra, real-time spin correlations, and dynamical structure factors. In preliminary results for open-boundary Heisenberg clusters, exact diagonalization provides controlled spectra and short-time correlation dynamics, while circuit simulations quantify Trotter error and resource requirements for shallow hardware implementations. We discuss how TDVP convergence, entanglement growth, and circuit depth define complementary reliability windows. The goal of this ongoing work is to identify spin-dynamical observables that are physically meaningful, classically verifiable, and suitable for near-term quantum-hardware experiments..
